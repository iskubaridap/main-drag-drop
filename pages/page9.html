This is the Shared Responsibility model, and under this model, some security concerns are the customer’s responsibility, some are the responsibility of the service provider, and some responsibilities are shared between the client and service provider. Some clients struggle to understand this model, which can lead to mistakes. Recent examples of serious data-loss incidents include FedEx in 2018 and Capital One in 2019.  Both leaks originated from Amazon Web Services, but were these incidents Amazon's fault?

In both cases, user error was the cause for the data loss incident. In FedEx’s case, they failed to configure an Amazon Simple Storage Service Bucket (or S3) setting exposing the private information of thousands of Customers. For Capital One, a misconfigured AWS Firewall, and subsequent compromise of an administrator password, allowed the exposure of 100 million individuals in the US and 6 million people in Canada.  They didn't understand their shared responsibility in the Cloud, and that lead to decisions that left them vulnerable. They are not alone.

Only 26% of companies say they can audit IaaS configurations.
